How a tuition payment actually goes

Same four steps whether it's tuition or a personal remittance. Only the destination account changes.

01

Compare

Tell us the amount and where you're sending it. We show you rates from a few banks and RBI-authorised exchange houses near you.

02

Book & verify

Pick a rate, book online, send over your KYC docs.

03

Transfer funds

You send the amount to the exchange house/bank as NEFT or RTGS — usual net banking, nothing unusual.

04

Delivered

We initiate the wire. University side usually shows the credit within 48 working hours.

Documents you'll need

  • University letter or prospectus showing the fee amount
  • Indian passport copy of the student
  • ID proof of the sender — Aadhaar, driving licence, voter ID, or passport
  • PAN card of the sender

The sender can be the student or a parent — the amount must come from the sender's own account.

Not sure which documents you need?

Send us your university's fee letter and we'll confirm exactly what's required for your specific transfer, before you start the KYC process.

Ask Us First

Questions we get a lot

Is there a minimum amount?

No fixed minimum for tuition payments. For personal remittance there's sometimes a practical floor depending on which exchange house gives the best rate that day — we'll tell you at the time.

Do you have a branch I can walk into?

We work through a network of RBI-authorised exchange house partners rather than running our own branches everywhere, so the nearest option depends on your location. Call us and we'll point you to the closest one.

What if my university needs the money faster than 48 hours?

Tell us upfront — some routes are faster than others depending on the destination country and receiving bank. We'll be honest if a tight deadline isn't realistic rather than promise something we can't deliver.

Can the sender be someone other than the student?

Yes — usually a parent. The money just needs to come from the sender's own bank account, and their ID/PAN go with the KYC.
